A cross-section of geologic time, ringed like tree rings and labelled on the real Geologic Time Scale. The rim is the present; travel inward through the Holocene → Pleistocene (both the Quaternary Period), through the Neogene & Paleogene — the Age of Mammals (Titanoboa, Megalodon, terror birds) — past the Cretaceous dinosaurs and down to the two deepest rings: the Cambrian explosion (the Burgess Shale) and, at the very core, the Ediacaran — the strange soft life before the animals. The rings are ordinal, not to scale, so the jump from the Cambrian out to the Cretaceous skips most of the Paleozoic.

Each slice is a place & ecosystem, so 🧬 Evolution draws spokes from an extinct animal out to its living relative on the rim.
