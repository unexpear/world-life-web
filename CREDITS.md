# Credits & Citations

World Life Web is an educational, non-commercial visualization. All data is drawn from the
open sources below; please retain these credits if you reuse it. Per-claim source links live in
[fact-check.md](fact-check.md).

## Software
- **Cytoscape.js** — graph rendering. Franz M, Lopes CT, Huck G, Dong Y, Sumer O, Bader GD (2016).
  *Cytoscape.js: a graph theory library for visualisation and analysis.* Bioinformatics 32(2):309–311.
  https://js.cytoscape.org/

## Featured published food web
- **Paine, R. T. (1966).** *Food Web Complexity and Species Diversity.* The American Naturalist,
  100(910), 65–75. https://doi.org/10.1086/282400
  — The Pacific rocky-intertidal (Mukkaw Bay) web; the study that coined **"keystone species."**
- Revisited by **Wootton, J. T., et al. (2016).** *Revisiting Paine's 1966 Sea Star Removal Experiment,
  the Most-Cited Empirical Article in the American Naturalist.* Am. Nat. 188(4). https://doi.org/10.1086/688045

## Taxonomy
- **GBIF** — GBIF Secretariat: GBIF Backbone Taxonomy. https://www.gbif.org
  (scientific-name, rank, and classification verification; every name confirmed as an exact match).

## Fossil ages & occurrences
- **The Paleobiology Database (PBDB).** https://paleobiodb.org
  (first/last-appearance ages for the extinct taxa; e.g. Smilodon, Titanoboa, Tyrannosaurus).

## Conservation status & present-day data
- **IUCN Red List of Threatened Species (2024).** https://www.iucnredlist.org
  (conservation status of each living species; global total of 47,187 threatened of 169,420 assessed).
- **World Bank Open Data (2021).** https://data.worldbank.org
  — Forest area, % of land (`AG.LND.FRST.ZS`) and Terrestrial protected areas, % of land
  (`ER.LND.PTLD.ZS`), world and by region. (World Bank re-publishes IUCN/UNEP-WCMC species data.)

## Key species facts & extinction science
- **Dalén, L., et al. (2024).** *Temporal dynamics of woolly mammoth genome erosion prior to extinction.*
  Cell. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cell.2024.05.033 — rejects the "inbreeding doomed them" hypothesis.
- **Higuchi, R., et al. (1984).** *DNA sequences from the quagga, an extinct member of the horse family.*
  Nature 312:282–284 — the first extinct animal to have its DNA sequenced.
- **Fulton, T. L., et al. (2012).** Passenger-pigeon phylogeny (*Patagioenas* is the closest relative).
- **Numbat genome (2022)** — identifies the numbat as the thylacine's closest living relative.
- Natural History Museum (London), Britannica, and cross-referenced Wikipedia/BirdLife DataZone articles
  for dodo, thylacine, great auk, Steller's sea cow, passenger pigeon, quagga, mammoth, Megatherium, and the
  Age-of-Mammals taxa (Titanoboa, Megalodon, Paraceratherium, Basilosaurus, Argentavis, Australopithecus).
- Diet/trophic data: La Brea stable-isotope studies (Smilodon), fossil-dung analyses (mammoth forbs),
  and standard references (passenger pigeon mast, great auk capelin). See [fact-check.md](fact-check.md).
