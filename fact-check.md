## Data sources & re-verification (GBIF · IUCN · PBDB · World Bank)
Cross-checked against authoritative databases:
- **GBIF** (taxonomy backbone) — every scientific name is an **EXACT** match. `Hydrodamalis gigas` → family
  **Dugongidae**, independently confirming the sea-cow → dugong fix. Notes: GBIF's backbone lists
  `Otodus megalodon` as a synonym of the older `Carcharias megalodon` (our name is the current consensus),
  and over-lumps `Argentavis` with vultures — the names themselves are correct.
- **Paleobiology Database (PBDB)** — confirmed fossil age ranges: Smilodon (Gelasian→Holocene), Titanoboa
  (~58 Ma), T. rex (Maastrichtian ~66 Ma). PBDB returned a noisy ~7 Ma "first appearance" for
  *M. primigenius* which we correctly reject (the woolly mammoth arose ~400 ka).
- **IUCN Red List (2024)** — living relatives tagged with real status (badge on each card). One correction:
  **Razorbill is Near Threatened, not Least Concern**. Global figure used in-app: **47,187 of 169,420
  assessed species threatened**. 7 of the 11 living relatives here are threatened (devil, numbat, dugong,
  sea otter, puffin, lion, Asian elephant).
- **World Bank (2021)** — present-day layer: world forest 31.2%, protected land 15.8%; per-region protected
  areas on ecosystem drill-downs (Sub-Saharan Africa 16.4%, Australia 20.4%, N. America 12.4%, Europe &
  C. Asia 15.6%).

## Verified correct across ≥2 sources (kept)
- Dodo's closest living relative = **Nicobar pigeon** (Shapiro 2002 mtDNA); sister species = Rodrigues solitaire — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dodo) · [BMC Evol. Biol.](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4099497/)
- Mammoth's closest living relative = **Asian elephant** (~95.8% mtDNA); Wrangel Island survival to **~2000 BC** — [Science News](https://www.sciencenews.org/article/mammoth-findings-asian-elephant-closest-living-kin) · [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2019/10/08/us/woolly-mammoths-death-scn-trnd/index.html)
- **Birds are living dinosaurs**, far closer to T. rex than crocodiles are — textbook consensus
- Quagga = **subspecies of plains zebra**; in **1984** it was the *first extinct animal to have its DNA sequenced* (Higuchi et al., Nature); last one died Amsterdam Zoo **12 Aug 1883** — [Nature 1984](https://www.nature.com/articles/312282a0) · [Quagga Project](https://www.quaggaproject.org/)
- Great auk: last pair killed **Eldey, Iceland, 3 June 1844** — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_auk) · [Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/animal/great-auk)
- Steller's sea cow: discovered **1741**, extinct **1768** (27 yrs); 8–9 m — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steller's_sea_cow)
- Passenger pigeon: last bird **Martha**, Cincinnati Zoo, **1 Sept 1914** — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Passenger_pigeon)

## Flagged as uncertain / debated (labelled as such in-app)
- **Mammoth mainland cause** — climate (shrinking steppe) vs human hunting is *still actively debated*; most evidence says both, combined. [ScienceDaily 2021](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2021/10/211020135914.htm)
- **Dodo ↔ tambalacoque tree coevolution** — the famous "the tree needs the dodo" claim (Temple 1977) is **largely discredited**. Kept only as a disputed ecological link.
- **Sabre-tooth relatives** — Machairodontinae has **no close living relative**; all modern cats are roughly equidistant. The lion link is labelled "distant."
- **Thylacine closest relative** — numbat vs Tasmanian devil is unresolved; both shown.
